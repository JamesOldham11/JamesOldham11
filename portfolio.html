Unsupervised Learning

I used daily average (Farenheit) temperatures from 1950 to 2022 at Heathrow on the hypothesis that, with global warming, we would see years chronoligcally close to each other being clustered together.

The results aren't surprising, with only three years since 2000 in the right two clusters that seem to correspond to cooler year clusters. I was surprised that none of the five hottest years were directly grouped together, which goes to show they must have had varying warmer periods during their respective years.
